Symptoms of Coronavirus

Related Pages

What you need to know

  • People with COVID-19 have had a wide range of symptoms reported – ranging from mild symptoms to severe illness.
  • Older adults and people who have severe underlying medical conditions like heart or lung disease or diabetes seem to be at higher risk for developing more serious complications from COVID-19 illness.
  • Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. People with these symptoms may have COVID-19

Symptoms of COVID-19

Please be familiar with the following range of symptoms, increasing in severity related to the virus as people with these symptoms may have COVID-19:

    Mild Symptoms
  • Dry and Sore Throat
  • Coughing - This can be any kind of cough, not just dry
  • Tiredness and a lack of energy for daily tasks.

    • People with mild symptoms who are otherwise healthy, should behave as if you have the virus and self-isolate for 14 days. People in your household will need to restrict their movements.


    Moderate Symptoms
  • Fever - High temperature of 38 degrees Celsius or above
  • Loss or Change to your sense of smell or taste - You cannot smell or taste anything
  • Loss or Change to your sense of smell or taste - Things smell or taste different to normal
  • Neurological symptoms such as different degrees of headaches
  • Lack of orientation, Decreased alertness, and muscle pain.
  • Conjunctivitis, a rash on skin, or discolouration of fingers or toes

    • Severe Symptoms
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Dizziness- Difficulty concentrating
  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Loss of speech or movement
  • Sudden digestive symptoms such as diarrhea

Call Emergency services and seek immediate medical attention if you have any of the serious symptoms to notify the operator that you are seeking care for someone who has or may have COVID-19.
You may also need to be tested for coronavirus. Contact your local GP to find out when you should call to be assessed for a test.


This list does not include all possible symptoms. HSE will continue to update this list as we learn more about COVID-19.
